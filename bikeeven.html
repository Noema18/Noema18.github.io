Cycle the Continental Divide

Cycle the Continental Divide is our annully hosted event for riders across the world to bike the best trail on earth, the The Great Divide Mountain Bike Route (GDMBR). GDMBR is the premier off-pavement cycling route, crisscrossing the Continental Divide north to south. This route is defined by the word "remote." Its remoteness equates with spectacular terrain and scenery. The entire route is basically dirt-road and mountain-pass riding every day. In total, it has over 200,000 feet of elevation gain. Roughly 80% of it is composed of county, Forest Service (USFS), and Bureau of Land Management (BLM) dirt and gravel roads. The remainder is made up of four-wheel-drive tracks and singletrack trails (10%), and paved roads (10%). Don't miss out on this incredible adventure biking some of the worlds most beautify scenery on this route from Canada to Mexico. Tours are 2754 miles long, biking around 100 miles per day for 30 days. Be sure you and your doctor agree you are healthy enough to undertake this adventure!
